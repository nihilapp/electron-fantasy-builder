## Getting Started

### What is Fantasy Builder?

Fantasy Builder is a desktop application designed for creative professionals to organize and manage fictional worlds, characters, and settings. Whether you're writing a novel, designing a game, or creating a comic, Fantasy Builder helps you maintain consistency and depth in your creative projects.

### First Launch

When you first open Fantasy Builder, you'll see a welcoming interface with options to:
- Create a new project
- Manage existing projects (if any)

The application features a dark/light theme that can be toggled for comfortable working in any lighting condition.

---

## Projects

### Creating a New Project

1. **Click "프로젝트 생성" (Create Project)** from the main screen
2. **Fill in project details:**
   - **Project Name** (required): The title of your creative work
   - **Genre**: Choose from Fantasy, Sci-Fi, Horror, Romance, etc.
   - **Description**: A brief summary of your project
   - **Explanation**: Detailed description of the world or story
3. **Click "생성" (Create)** to initialize your project

### Managing Projects

From the project management screen, you can:
- **View all projects** with their metadata
- **Open existing projects** to work on them
- **Edit project information** (name, genre, description)
- **Delete projects** (soft deleted, can be recovered)

### Project Navigation

Each project has a dedicated workspace with sections for:
- **Overview**: Project summary and statistics
- **Settings**: Project-wide configuration
- **Traits & Abilities**: Character attributes and skills
- **Core Rules**: World-building rules and systems
- **Entities**: Characters, creatures, locations, etc.

## Core Concepts

### Traits (특성)

Traits are characteristics, qualities, or attributes that define entities in your world.

#### Types of Traits
- **Personality Traits**: Brave, cunning, compassionate
- **Physical Traits**: Strong, fast, tall
- **Background Traits**: Noble, orphan, educated
- **Magical Traits**: Telepathic, enchanted, cursed

#### Creating Traits
1. Navigate to **Traits & Abilities** section
2. Choose whether to create a **Global Trait** (shared across projects) or **Project Trait** (project-specific)
3. **Fill in trait details:**
   - **Name**: Clear, descriptive trait name
   - **Category**: Large and medium classification
   - **Application Targets**: Who can have this trait (Character, Creature, Organization)
   - **Description**: What this trait means in your world
   - **Conflicts**: Traits that conflict with this one

#### Trait Categories
- **Large Categories**: Personality, Physical, Background, Social, Magical
- **Medium Categories**: More specific sub-categories within each large category

### Abilities (능력)

Abilities are skills, powers, or capabilities that entities can perform or possess.

#### Types of Abilities
- **Combat Abilities**: Sword mastery, archery, martial arts
- **Magical Abilities**: Fire spells, healing, divination
- **Social Abilities**: Persuasion, leadership, intimidation
- **Crafting Abilities**: Blacksmithing, alchemy, enchanting

#### Creating Abilities
1. Navigate to **Traits & Abilities** section
2. Select **Abilities** tab
3. **Choose Global or Project scope**
4. **Fill in ability details:**
   - **Name**: Clear ability name
   - **Type**: Magic, Combat, Social, Crafting, etc.
   - **Category**: Specific classification
   - **Description**: How the ability works
   - **Target Type**: Who/what it affects (Ally, Enemy, Self)
   - **Damage Type**: Fire, Physical, Mental, etc.

## Entity Management

### Characters

Characters are the people who inhabit your world.

#### Character Profile
1. **Basic Information**
   - Name and alias/nickname
   - Role type (Protagonist, Antagonist, Supporting)
   - One-line description (logline)
   - Race/species
   - Nationality
   - Affiliation (organizations)

2. **Assigning Traits & Abilities**
   - Select from global traits/abilities
   - Add project-specific traits/abilities
   - Define character's unique combination

3. **Physical & Personal Details**
   - Age, gender, appearance
   - Background story
   - Motivations and goals

### Creatures & Races

Creatures include both races and specific species in your world.

#### Creating Creatures
1. **Basic Information**
   - Species/race name
   - Creature type (Humanoid, Beast, Undead, etc.)
   - Physical description
   - Habitat and behavior

2. **Assign Traits & Abilities**
   - Racial traits common to all members
   - Specific abilities unique to this creature

3. **Cultural Information** (for intelligent species)
   - Social structure
   - Beliefs and values
   - Relationship with other races

### Organizations

Organizations are groups, factions, companies, or social structures.

#### Organization Types
- **Political**: Kingdoms, empires, republics
- **Military**: Armies, mercenary groups, knightly orders
- **Economic**: Merchant guilds, trading companies
- **Religious**: Churches, cults, philosophical schools
- **Criminal**: Thieves' guilds, smuggling rings

#### Organization Profile
- Name and type
- Leadership structure
- Membership requirements
- Goals and methods
- Relations with other organizations

### Locations

Locations include regions, nations, and specific places in your world.

#### Geographic Hierarchy
1. **Regions**: Large geographical areas
2. **Nations**: Political entities within regions
3. **Specific Places**: Cities, dungeons, landmarks

#### Location Details
- Name and type
- Geographic features
- Climate and environment
- Population and demographics
- Political control
- Historical significance

### Items

Items are objects of significance in your world.

#### Item Categories
- **Weapons**: Swords, staves, firearms
- **Armor**: Shields, plate, magical protection
- **Tools**: Lockpicks, alchemical equipment
- **Artifacts**: Objects of great power
- **Consumables**: Potions, food, magical items

#### Item Properties
- Name and type
- Physical description
- Function and usage
- Materials and craftsmanship
- Historical significance
- Associated abilities or powers

## Core Rules

Core rules define the fundamental systems that govern your world.

### Types of Core Rules

#### Magic Systems
- How magic works
- Sources of power
- Limitations and costs
- Schools of magic
- Magical creatures and items

#### Technology Systems
- Available technology level
- Technological limitations
- Specialized knowledge
- Resource requirements

#### Social Systems
- Government types
- Legal systems
- Social hierarchies
- Cultural norms
- Economic systems

#### Physical Laws
- Unique physics (if applicable)
- Natural phenomena
- Climate and weather patterns
- Geographical features

#### Creating Core Rules
1. Navigate to **Core Rules** section
2. **Add new rule:**
   - Rule name
   - Rule type
   - Detailed explanation
   - Examples and applications
3. **Organize rules** into logical groups
4. **Cross-reference** rules with related entities

## Relationships

### Entity Relationships

Fantasy Builder allows you to define relationships between different entities:

#### Character Relationships
- **Family**: Parent, child, sibling, cousin
- **Social**: Friend, rival, mentor, student
- **Professional**: Colleague, boss, employee
- **Political**: Ally, enemy, vassal, sovereign

#### Organization Relationships
- **Political**: Alliance, war, trade agreement
- **Hierarchical**: Parent organization, branch
- **Competitive**: Rivalry, competition

#### Location Relationships
- **Political**: Capital, colony, occupied territory
- **Geographic**: Neighbor, contains, borders
- **Trade**: Trade routes, economic ties

### Creating Relationships
1. **Select entity** you want to define relationships for
2. **Choose relationship type**
3. **Select target entity**
4. **Define relationship details:**
   - Nature of relationship
   - Start date (if applicable)
   - Current status
   - Notes and context
